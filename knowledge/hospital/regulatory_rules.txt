REGULATORY COMPLIANCE RULES - General Hospital

PRESCRIPTION VALIDATION RULES (REQUIRED for supervisor approval):
  R-1: Every prescription must contain: patient ID, date, prescribing doctor ID,
       drug name, dose, route of administration, frequency, and clinical rationale.
  R-2: Allergy contraindication check is mandatory. If the prescribed drug matches
       a known patient allergy, prescription must be REJECTED and doctor notified.
  R-3: Drug-drug interaction check required. Major interactions (per Lexicomp/Micromedex)
       must be documented and supervisor-approved before dispensing.
  R-4: Schedule II controlled substances require supervisor co-signature.
  R-5: Off-formulary drugs require pharmacy director pre-authorization before supervisor approval.

PATIENT SAFETY RULES:
  S-1: No PHI may leave the hospital system boundary without explicit patient consent.
  S-2: Allergy information must be verified against the allergy database, not assumed from memory.
  S-3: For any prescription flagged with a contraindication, the workflow must STOP and
       ESCALATE to the supervising physician before proceeding.
  S-4: Pediatric dosing requires weight-based calculation review before approval.

SLA THRESHOLDS:
  - Prescription turnaround (non-urgent): 2 hours from order to dispensing
  - Prescription turnaround (urgent/STAT): 30 minutes
  - Allergy check response: < 5 minutes
  - Supervisor co-signature (controlled): < 1 hour

COMPLIANCE STANDARDS:
  - HIPAA: All patient data handling must comply with 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164.
  - Joint Commission: Medication reconciliation required at every care transition.
  - State Board of Pharmacy: All dispensing must be documented in the pharmacy system.
